Introduction to Measurement and Uncertainty

Measurement is a fundamental aspect of scientific inquiry. It involves the process of assigning numerical values to physical quantities, providing a quantitative basis for understanding the natural world.

However, no measurement is entirely free from uncertainty. Uncertainty arises due to limitations in measurement devices, experimental conditions, and inherent variability in the measured quantity.

Key concepts related to measurement and uncertainty include:

  • Precision: The degree of repeatability or reproducibility of measurements.
  • Accuracy: The closeness of a measurement to the true or accepted value.
  • Uncertainty: The range within which the true value of a measurement is likely to lie.
  • Significant Figures: Digits in a measured value that carry meaningful information.

Scientists use various tools and statistical methods to quantify and account for uncertainty, ensuring that measurements are reliable and meaningful.
